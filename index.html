A Brief History

Christ Church Girls' School is one of the two remaining all girls' primary schools in Barbados. The school has its origins in the Kingsland Girls' School which was opened in 1898 on the Kingsland Estates. In 1921 the school was moved to its present location and in 1926 it was amalgamated with the Providence Girls' School to form the Christ Church Girls' School.

Over the years, the Christ Church Girls' School has been fortunate to be under the leadership and knowledge of foward-looking principals including Miss Hewitt and Miss Eugenia Carter-Hinds. The latter whose tenure lasted from 1944-68 is credited with composing the School Song, instituting the school's P.T.A. in 1954 and initiating the school's Savings Co-operative (the first in the island)in 1968. Other principals were Mrs. Amerel Haynes, Miss Adele Smith, Mrs. Glenis Nurse, Mrs. Coral Padmore and Mrs. Heather Bryan.

Rules and Regulations

  • There must be no playing before morning assembly, during break-time or on the hard pavement around the school.
  • Every day, notice bells will be rung at 8:30 a.m. and 12:45 p.m. These notify the pupils to prepare for assembly. All buying of snacks should cease when these bells are wrung.
  • All pupils should be in the line from 8:40 a.m. and 12:55 p.m. respectively
  • Teachers are responsible for pupils from8:30 a.m. until 3:15 p.m.Ensure your child's safety. All pupils except those who are givenspecial permission must be off the premises by 3:15 p.m.
  • On entering the compound on mornings, no pupil may leave the school premises until the end of school, except with the special permission of the principal.
  • At the sound of the bell during school sessions, there must be silence.
  • On no account must there be any running through the school or the pavement.
  • No pupil is permitted to use the corridor outside the Principal's office unless it is ranging or on business in the office.
  • There must be no writing on the desks, chairs or walls.
  • All refuse should be placed in waste paper baskets or bins.
  • Keep the premises and classrooms tidy.
  • All pupils are expected to be regular and punctual in their attendance.
  • All pupils are expected to give the Principal, teachers and other members of staff due respect and cooperation and generally to behave in a manner that will bring credit to themselves and the school.
  • There should be no fighting in classrooms, on the school premises or along the street.
